Campground

We're staying at The Great Sand Dunes Oasis, located just a couple miles outside the park entrance. We have Group Sites 2 & 3 on the upper loop — primitive sites only.

Good for tents and maybe small travel trailers, but the road is tight and rough. Don't bring anything large — it may be damaged by trees/brush and difficult to fit.

Check-In

Everyone must check in at the main office before proceeding to camp. There is a $7/person charge. Tell them you're with the Black Forest church group — reservation is also under Evan's name.

Arriving after 7:00 PM? The office may be closed. There will be directions posted on the office door to Group Sites 2 & 3. Please stop by the next morning to pay.

Great Sand Dunes National Park

Hopefully Medano Creek will be flowing! A National Park permit is required to enter. There's a large parking lot where you can hike to the dunes or stage a vehicle before heading further in.

Castle Creek Picnic Area

Our Saturday destination — great spot with picnic tables and bathrooms where we can play in the creek and sand. Located 2.6 miles down the Medano Pass Primitive Road.

4WD required (NOT all-wheel drive) with high clearance. You may need to air down tires — there's an air compressor at the start of the primitive road. Parking is very limited — consolidate vehicles at the main lot and go early!
